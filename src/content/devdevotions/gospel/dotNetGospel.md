Historically, fathers have had the power and the primary responsibility to shape the lives of their children. 

They are to be shapers of character and faithfulness and to bring their children up in the "Fear of the Lord." 

Fathers are to be loving, patient, and wise in their guidance, ensuring that they do not provoke their children to anger through harshness or neglect. 

Instead, they are called to nurture their children with discipline and instruction that reflects the teachings of God, fostering an environment where children can grow in faith and character.

Earthly Fathers often fail to do this perfectly.
And as Children look to their earthly fathers for dependence, sustainance and guidance in loving relationship, we are all to look to our Heavenly Father who has and is shaping us into the image of His Son, Jesus Christ. If we are in fact, in him.
